Daily & Off-Day Work

Ben Neely · Shoulder health · GTG · Mobility · Every day counts

Every day — no exceptions

Every day
Doorframe pec stretch
30 sec / side
throughout day
Hands at shoulder height on both sides of any doorframe. Lean gently through until you feel the chest open. Hold 30 seconds.

Why: Pec minor restriction is pulling your shoulders forward. Your wife is seeing this. Two weeks of KB pressing on top of desk posture accelerates it. This is the direct fix.

Do it every time you walk through a doorframe at home. Zero time cost.
Every day
Band pull-aparts
20–30 reps
1 set
Hold band at shoulder height, arms straight, pull apart until band touches chest. Controlled return. Search "band pull-apart" — 30 seconds of video covers it.

Why: Direct antagonist to your pressing volume. Rear deltoid and mid-trap activation. Best single movement for shoulder rounding correction.

Morning or desk break. Band lives somewhere visible so you don't forget it.
Every day
Hip flexor wall stretch
60–90 sec / side
Rear knee on floor, front foot forward, drive hips forward and squeeze glute of rear leg. Hold.

Why: Desk hip flexors shut off glute activation — the foundation of every ABC complex, carry, and snatch you're doing. Highest ROI stretch for surf performance too.

Morning at home. Takes 3 minutes total.

Off-day GTG — hall pullup bar at home

Off days only
Pullup GTG
1–3 reps
every pass
Every time you walk past the hall bar — 1 to 3 pullups. Never to failure. Never when arms are already tired. Just grease the pattern.

Why: Your push-to-pull ratio is imbalanced. Press ladders + pushups significantly outvolume your pullups each session. GTG on off days builds pull volume without taxing the pressing muscles.

Dan John's most repeated recommendation for adding pullup reps. It works because it's never a workout — just practice.

On KB session days: skip this. Don't add GTG on top of Day B — that's how the elbow creeps up.
Off days only
Dead hang
20–30 sec
1–2 sets
Passive hang from the hall bar. Full grip, shoulders relaxed and letting the weight decompress the spine and shoulder. Don't active-shrug.

Why: Best passive elbow rehab tool you have. Decompresses the joint, opens the shoulder, stretches the lat. Directly addresses medial epicondylitis recovery.

Pair with pullup GTG — hang after your reps.

Home GTG — available anytime

Every day
Goblet squat GTG
5 reps / pass
12kg bell
5 slow goblet squats with the 12kg whenever you walk past the bell at home. Pause at the bottom. Keep the bell at home for this purpose.

Why: Greases the front squat pattern that's in every ABC complex. Keeps hips and ankles mobile between sessions.

Bell lives somewhere you walk past it. Out of sight = forgotten.

On KB session days

KB days
Warmup only — no GTG
~5–7 min
Wrist circles → forearm self-massage → halos → prying goblet squats → plyometric squats → hip flexor wall stretch → dead hang if available.

Daily mobility (pec stretch, band pull-aparts) still applies — just do them earlier in the day, not as part of warmup.

No pullup GTG on KB days. Elbow protection trumps volume accumulation.
